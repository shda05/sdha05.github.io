NUMBERS

In Korea, they have 2 systems of number: Sino-Korean & Native-Korean.
Sino-Korean is based on Chinese numbers.

  • Sino-Korean: For counting large numbers, money, number in postal address, phone numbers, date, minute, lenth, area, weight, volume, %, integer.
  • Native-Korean: For counting number less than 100, counting objects other than money.

There are 2 ways to say 0, which are 공 & 영.

  • 영 is used in mathematics & counting.
  • 공 is used to say phone numbers & called the numbers individually.

Example:-
  1. Phone numbers: 012-3456789 (공일이 삼사오육칠팔구)
  2. Called the numbers individually: 2020 (이공이공)
